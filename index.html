Timeline

1968
Intel Founded

Intel was founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, who worked together at Fairchild Semiconductor in San Jose, California, with the determination to make memory chips smaller, dependable, and high performance.

1971
First Microprocessor
Model: Intel 4004

Intel produced and introduced the first commercial microprocessor (Intel 4004), a 4-bit processor that helped spark modern computing innovation. The Intel 4004 was discontinued in 1982.

1978
Intel 8086
Model: Intel 8086

Intel introduced the Intel 8086 Microprocessor, which helped start the x86 innovation into personal computers with a 16-bit processor. It was discontinued in 1998.

1985
Intel 80386
Model: Intel 80386

Intel introduced the Intel 80386, a major 32-bit processor advancement with 275K transistors. It was incorporated into many personal computers and helped drive performance forward. It was discontinued in 2007.

2006
Intel Core 2 Duo
Model: Intel Core 2 Duo

Intel introduced the Intel Core 2 Duo microprocessor, a 64-bit CPU that improved performance and efficiency for personal computers and laptops. It was discontinued in 2008.

2020
2020 Product Advances
Still Active: Not discontinued

Intel introduces a few products including the Intel Atom PS900, the Intel 3rd Gen Xeon Scalable, and the 10nm-based 11th Gen Intel Core.

  • Intel Atom PS900: primarily built for low power consumption and embedded-style applications on netbooks.
  • 3rd Gen Xeon Scalable: could achieve 80 cores, built for high workloads and performance with power efficiency.
  • 11th Gen Intel Core: built for high resolution with graphics.

All three processors are still being used to this day and not discontinued. Another fact is that Intel was at the frontlines of AI and 5G production.
